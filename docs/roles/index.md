# Role guides

These guides explain decisions and workflows, not merely skill names. One person
may perform several roles, but every material gate still needs one locally named
accountable owner. The labels are repository conventions; adapt them through a
documented responsibility assignment matrix.

| Role | Primary concern | Guide |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Trainee developer | Learn safely and recognize completion | [Trainee developer](trainee-developer.md) |
| Software engineer | Implement a bounded, testable change | [Software engineer](software-engineer.md) |
| Lead developer | Technical direction, review, and merge safety | [Lead developer](lead-developer.md) |
| Quality assurance (QA) engineer | Independent quality and regression evidence | [QA engineer](qa-engineer.md) |
| Business analyst | Actors, workflows, rules, exceptions, and requirements | [Business analyst](business-analyst.md) |
| Product owner | Backlog clarity, ordering, and increment acceptance within delegated authority | [Product owner](product-owner.md) |
| Product manager | Customer problem, outcomes, scope, and product trade-offs | [Product manager](product-manager.md) |
| Delivery manager | Cross-role sequencing, dependencies, and impediments | [Delivery manager](delivery-manager.md) |
| Engineering manager | People, capacity, engineering controls, and team adoption | [Engineering manager](engineering-manager.md) |
| Vice President (VP) of Engineering | Portfolio adoption, operating model, investment, and risk | [VP of Engineering](vp-engineering.md) |
| Chief Technology Officer (CTO) | Technical strategy and organizational adoption approval | [CTO](cto.md) |
| Development and operations (DevOps) engineer | Reproducible platforms, continuous integration and continuous delivery (CI/CD), release, and recovery | [DevOps engineer](devops-engineer.md) |
| Security reviewer | Threats, data, permissions, and residual risk | [Security reviewer](security-reviewer.md) |

Use [Skills by role](../reference/skills-by-role.md) after choosing a job. That
reference is a capability lookup; these pages are the operating guides.
