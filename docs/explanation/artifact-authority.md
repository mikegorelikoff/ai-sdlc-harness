---
title: Artifact authority
description: Which records are authoritative, which are projections or proposals, and why review tools cannot silently rewrite delivery truth.
---

Delivery systems become unsafe when every generated summary appears equally authoritative. The harness assigns distinct roles to source artifacts, machine projections, evidence reports, and proposals.

## Authoritative detail

Requirements, design, accepted decisions, test cases, QA scope, task definitions, and approved policy live in visible repository artifacts owned by the responsible role. Changes follow their normal workflow and preserve history.

## Derived control records

Indexes, TOON plans, context packs, and compatibility snapshots accelerate routing and validation. They project source truth and carry freshness identity. When a projection disagrees with its source, refresh the projection; do not quietly edit the source to match.

## Advisory artifacts

Quality lenses, evidence councils, research, change-impact reports, and retrospectives produce findings or proposals. Their value comes from evidence and traceability, not authority. An owner must accept a proposal before it changes a requirement, policy, or release decision.

This boundary lets automation be ambitious without pretending that generation equals approval.
