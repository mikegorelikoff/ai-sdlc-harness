# Readiness Checklist

Review whether:

- the customer problem is clear;
- the target customer is specific;
- the value proposition is compelling;
- the business goals are measurable;
- the MVP is clearly defined;
- the out-of-scope items are explicit;
- the requirements are linked to business value;
- the user scenarios are specific;
- the acceptance criteria are testable;
- the risks are realistic;
- the assumptions are clearly marked;
- the open questions are actionable;
- contradictions remain;
- anything important is missing.

## Readiness Scale

- 1-3: Too vague for serious alignment
- 4-6: Basic direction exists but major gaps remain
- 7-8: Good enough for discovery alignment and early design
- 9-10: Strong enough for stakeholder alignment and delivery handoff

## Final Review Output

Explain:

- why the score was given;
- what is strong;
- what is weak;
- what must be clarified next;
- what should happen before design or development starts.

## When To Load This Reference

Load when reviewing PRFAQ, BRD, product brief, discovery notes, or business
context for readiness before design, delivery, backlog, or implementation work.

## Scoring Dimensions

| Dimension | Strong Evidence | Weak Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Customer/problem | specific segment and pain | generic user/problem |
| Value | measurable business/user value | vague benefit |
| Scope | MVP and non-goals clear | everything is in scope |
| Scenarios | concrete workflows | abstract capability list |
| Acceptance | observable outcomes | "works" language |
| Risks | specific and owned | generic risk list |
| Decisions | captured in log | implied or missing |

## Readiness Levels

- Not ready: core problem, customer, or scope is unclear.
- Partially ready: direction exists but major blockers remain.
- Ready with assumptions: work can proceed if assumptions are visible.
- Ready: downstream work can proceed with traceable evidence.

## Quick Flow Guidance

In `--quick-flow`, score the artifact and list only the gaps that materially
affect next-step work. Use assumptions for minor missing detail.

## Full Flow Guidance

In `--full-flow`, verify every scoring dimension and ask questions when a weak
dimension would cause downstream teams to invent scope.

## Decision Log Guidance

Record decisions for readiness verdict, accepted assumptions, deferred gaps, and
scope boundaries.
