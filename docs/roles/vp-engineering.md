# Vice President (VP) of Engineering guide

This guide uses continuous integration (CI).

## Why you should care

The harness changes how teams preserve intent and prove delivery. Scaling it
without fleet controls, ownership, and outcome evidence can multiply ceremony
and support cost.

## Where you participate

Approve evaluation scope, operating model, investment, policy minimums,
multi-team rollout, support ownership, metrics, and stop/hold/scale decisions.

## Inputs and outputs

Inputs: legal and security review, technical fit, pilot baseline and outcomes,
cost, support capacity, host/repository matrix, incidents, and team feedback.
Outputs: investment decision, rollout cohorts, minimum controls, exception
authority, portfolio metrics, and decommission criteria.

## Decisions you own

Own engineering-organization adoption and investment within executive policy.
Legal, compliance, and enterprise risk owners retain their authorities.

## Common mistakes

- Treating successful installation as organizational readiness.
- Comparing unlike teams with one blended productivity score.
- Scaling before license, support, version, CI, and fleet drift are controlled.
- Failing to budget human review and platform ownership.

## Example workflow

Require legal and security clearance; run one bounded pilot; compare mechanism,
delivery, experience, and total-cost measures; test two repositories and an
upgrade/rollback; fund central ownership; promote by cohort with independent
stop conditions.

## Review checklist

- License and support boundaries are acceptable.
- Stable docs match the installed release.
- Multi-repository version and policy drift are measurable.
- Consumer CI and rollback were exercised.
- Metrics avoid individual ranking and unsupported causality.
- Every rollout cohort can stop independently.
