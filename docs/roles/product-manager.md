# Product manager guide

This guide uses minimum viable product (MVP) and Press Release/Frequently Asked
Questions (PRFAQ).

## Why you should care

The harness makes assumptions, value, scope, and change decisions durable so a
substituted product interpretation becomes visible and reviewable when the
documented controls are followed.

## Where you participate

Lead working-backwards discovery, define audience and outcome, prioritize
problems, approve scope and non-goals, resolve product trade-offs, collaborate on
goals and release slices, and evaluate pilot results.

## Inputs and outputs

Inputs: customer and operational evidence, strategy, constraints, risks,
stakeholder positions, and success measures. Outputs: accepted discovery
package, product requirements, priorities, scope and change decisions, and
measurable pilot or release outcomes.

## Decisions you own

Own customer problem, outcome, strategic priority, and product trade-offs under
local policy. A delegated Product Owner may manage daily ordering and increment
acceptance; make that delegation explicit.

## Common mistakes

- Asking the agent to invent missing customer evidence.
- Treating a generated PRFAQ or gap count as stakeholder acceptance.
- Using universal numeric priority scores without a local rubric.
- Changing scope in chat without impact analysis and a durable decision.

## Example workflow

Start with a vague request; run discovery; separate facts, hypotheses, and open
questions; resolve stakeholder conflict through the accountable owner; approve
MVP and non-goals; hand off ready goals and requirements; when scope changes,
record a proposed decision, run impact analysis, approve or reject, and reopen
affected stages before implementation continues.

## Review checklist

- Customer, problem, evidence, outcome, and success measure are explicit.
- Assumptions and conflicting positions have owners and validation plans.
- MVP, post-MVP, non-goals, risks, and constraints are distinct.
- Priority is a human decision supported by visible criteria.
- Acceptance and change decisions link to repository evidence.
- Outcome metrics are not presented as causal proof without a valid study.
