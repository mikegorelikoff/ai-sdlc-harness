# Chief Technology Officer (CTO) guide

This guide uses artificial intelligence (AI), continuous integration (CI), and operating system (OS).

## Why you should care

Adoption creates a new repository control layer with AI, package, provenance,
permission, and maintenance implications. The CTO evaluates whether its vision
and controls justify that institutional dependency.

## Where you participate

Review purpose, architecture, licensing, security, portability, release model,
maintainership, integration strategy, enterprise governance, and evidence from
the bounded pilot.

## Inputs and outputs

Inputs: architecture and threat model, legal approval, support and ownership,
dependency/provenance plan, host conformance, pilot outcomes, and residual risk.
Outputs: approve, approve with conditions, or reject; required controls; named
executive owner; review date; and decommission or replacement criteria.

## Decisions you own

Own technical-strategy recommendation and adoption conditions within corporate
governance. Do not substitute for legal, privacy, compliance, or business
acceptance owners.

## Common mistakes

- Approving vendor-neutral claims without host conformance evidence.
- Accepting unsigned or mutable supply-chain inputs without documented risk.
- Ignoring maintenance cost of mirrored runtime and generated references.
- Treating workflow evidence as a correctness or compliance guarantee.

## Example workflow

Require a clean install and real workflow; inspect source/installed contract
parity; review legal rights, security reporting, provenance, CI, portability,
upgrade, and rollback; examine pilot evidence; approve only bounded scope until
High findings are closed.

## Review checklist

- Purpose, non-goals, and architectural authority are clear.
- Legal reuse rights and contribution ownership are explicit.
- Public docs, releases, and machine contracts align.
- Host, OS, permission, and data boundaries are tested and named.
- Maintenance, security response, and deprecation have owners.
- The final readiness decision matches unresolved risk.
