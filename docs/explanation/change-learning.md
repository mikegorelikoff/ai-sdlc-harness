---
title: Change and learning
description: How impact analysis and retrospectives improve the delivery system without rewriting history or policy automatically.
---

Delivery plans are hypotheses. Requirements change, architecture constraints emerge, tests disprove assumptions, and teams learn from execution. The harness treats that learning as governed evidence rather than failure of the original plan.

## Change impact

When an authoritative input changes, trace links identify downstream artifacts and stages that may be stale. A reopen proposal names evidence, owners, and the earliest affected stage. Previous conclusions remain visible.

## Retrospectives

Retrospectives separate observations from interpretations and improvement proposals. A proposal states expected value, cost, risk, owner, and validation path. It does not change configuration or workflow policy by itself.

## Controlled adoption

Accepted decisions turn proposals into work. Compatibility and validation then test whether the improvement behaves as intended. This prevents one unusual delivery from silently becoming a universal rule while still letting the system evolve from evidence.
